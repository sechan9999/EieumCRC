Our Mission & Vision

Mission & Vision

🎯 Our Mission

Eieum Civil Rights Center organizes Korean and Asian American immigrants in Atlanta, Georgia to achieve social, racial, and economic justice. Through data-driven advocacy, legal and social service connections, and community history preservation, we protect the rights of immigrant communities.

🌏 Our Vision

We envision a world where low-income immigrants, people of color, undocumented individuals, and youth become agents of change for a more just and equitable society. Our liberation is linked to the liberation of all marginalized communities.

"Eieum (以音) means connecting through sound. We amplify voices that have been silenced, creating ripples of change across communities."

Our Core Values

Core Values

Eieum Civil Rights Center is built upon four core values that guide everything we do.

⚖️

A commitment to fighting for justice. We stand with the most marginalized — recent immigrants, undocumented individuals, youth, women, and elders — and nurture their leadership.

💪

3 Core Projects

3 Core Projects

Launching in 2026, we implement three core projects in phases.

Project 1.1

Data-Driven Advocacy

Ph.1
Jul–Aug 2026 · Preparation
Survey & Database Design

Build standardized surveys and databases for collecting civil rights violation cases. Establish research framework and coordinate with partner organizations.

Ph.2
Sep–Nov 2026 · Research
Metro Atlanta Immigrant Survey

Conduct hybrid online/offline surveys. Drive broad participation through community centers, churches, and social media.

Ph.3
Dec 2026 · Analysis & Publication
2026 Atlanta Immigrant Civil Rights Report

Publish report with data analysis and policy recommendations. Publicize through press conferences and public hearings.

Expected Impact: Enhanced credibility of policy proposals through objective data and public discourse on civil rights issues
Project 1.2

Legal & Social Services Connection

🏛️

Legal Clinic Operations

Bi-weekly legal consultation days. Pro bono attorney network providing free legal advice on immigration and labor law.

📞

Multilingual Hotline

Multilingual hotline (Korean, English, Spanish) for emergency civil rights reporting and social service guidance. 365-day emergency response system.

📋

Case Management

Personalized case management beyond information provision, tracking outcomes and implementing continuous improvements through data.

Expected Impact: Practical rights protection for underserved populations and improved access to government services
Project 1.3

Community History Archive & Leadership Training

🎙️ 'Our Voices' Oral History Project

Preserving lives and struggles of first-generation immigrants through video and text. Building a living archive of Atlanta Korean immigrant history.

🌟 Eieum Leadership Academy

Civil rights history and organizing skills training for youth and next-gen activists. Held twice annually covering community organizing, policy advocacy, and Korean American civil rights history.

💻 Digital Archive Platform

Building an open web platform for oral histories, photos, and documents. Partnering with local universities for academic research.

Expected Impact: Strengthening community identity and building a foundation for sustainable civil rights movement
